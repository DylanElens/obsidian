Types of regulation
1. Operational regulation
2. Regulation by design
3. Regulation by control ^peNREUuo

Operational regulation
We need to assume that
    1. Disturbances are fixed
    2. Regulatory potential is fixed
    3. The concrete system(infrastructure) does not chagne
    4. The goals are fixed(norm values and Essential variables)

Operational regulations means applying regulatory actions(as given by the concrete system)
in the face of disturbances given the above assumptions. ^SjFBx5ch

Effective vs Efficient operational regulation

Effective -> For every disturbance there is a regulatory action
             that makes sure that the desired values of EV can
             be reached
Efficient ->  If you can be effective with as few regulatory actions
             as possible ^mO2f7e8N

Passive vs active
Passive -> Regulatory action is not selected by a regulator given some
           some disturbance, it is built in the system and works automatically
active  -> A regulator has to select a particular regulatory action for a 
           particular disturbance. A choice BETWEEN regulatory actions has 
           to be made
 ^Oy0prMWE

Error vs cause controlled active regulation
Error-controlled -> The regulatory reacts to damage 
                    and the goals is recovering from damage
                    The distrubance already has an effect
                    on the EV"s
Cause-controlled -> Here the regulator reacts to a threat.
                    The goals is to prevent the damage to the
                    EV's ^IgrVGV9q

Attenuation
Decrease the probability of the occurrence of a disturbance
P(D) = 0 ideally. ^DBstWFPG

3 ways of attenuation
1. elimination
    -> Remove Disturbance from the environment.
        e.g. arrest all who make virusses
2. Neutralization
    -> Change the infrastructure or relation with
       environment in such a way that some disturbance
       is is not longer a Disturbance for the system
       Although the disturbance still exists
3. Change infrastructure
    -> change the infrastructure in such a way
        that it itself is no longer a disturbance
        (e.g. Educate workers) ^YyykhCMa

Amplification means changing the infrastructure

Autopoietic systems

Autopoietic systems are 'self-producing' systems.
That is, an autopoietic system consists out of elements
that interact with each other and by means of their
interaction they product elements the system consist of.
->
As the interacting elements of an autopoietic system produce 
elements of the system, they also produce the system as a whole, 
that is, by means of their interactions these elements secure the
survival of the system in its environment.

    2. A system must be able to "persist" itself
    3. A system must be able to enlarge itself ^lbGXMoyh
